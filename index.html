Dr. Jean-Jacques Muyembe

The Man Who Saved a Billion Lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Muyembe's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Bandundu, DRC

  • 1960 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Lovanium, where he became interested in microbiology and graduated in 1969.

  • 1973 - He became a doctorate in virology at the University of Louvain KUL (Belgium), where he worked on viral infections using mouse models3. Returned to the Democratic Republic of the Congo in 1973, he worked in the fight against epidemics.

  • 1974 - A cholera epidemic in Matadi was the first crisis he worked on.

  • 1976- He was part of the team from the Yambuku Catholic Mission Hospital that investigated the first Ebola outbreak in.

  • 1978 - Jean-Jacques Muyembe was appointed dean of the Kinshasa University Medical School.

  • 1981 - He joined the Institut Pasteur in Dakar (Senegal), where he collaborated with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to study the Ebola and Marburg viruses.

  • 1995 - Having recognized the socio-cultural challenges of Ebola, he tries to encourage hospitals to fight infections better and to work together With David L. Heymann, he studies the Ebola epidemic.

  • 1998 - He was appointed director of the National Institute for Biomedical Research (INRB) of the Democratic Republic of Congo.

  • 2009 - It proves that the Ebola epidemics in the DRC can be attributed to exposure to Pteropodidae, a family of bats10. He then developed a serum treatment against Ebola by synthesizing antibodies from a person cured during a previous epidemic.

  • 2015 - Jean-Jacques Muyembe "received the Christophe Mérieux Prize for continuing his research in the Congo Basin." The same year, he received the Royal Society Africa Prize "for his seminal work on viral haemorrhagic fevers, including Ebola, which has helped to better understand the epidemiology and clinical manifestations of viral infections and to fight against their appearance."

  • 2017 - He joined forces with the Japan International Cooperation Agency to build a research complex with several biosafety laboratories.

  • 2018 - There "was another Ebola outbreak against which the fight was delayed due to delays in reporting." He is the first to use an experimental vaccine against Ebola to limit the spread of the virus, "in particular by vaccinating health professionals."

  • 2020 -He was appointed by President Félix Tshisekedi as coordinator of the response unit to the Covid-19 pandemic in the DRC.

"Dr Jean Jacques Muyembe is a Congolese citizen and a medical colleague for whom I have a lot of respect. His fight against Ebola, his help in the discovery of the virus in 1976 through the recent from 2018 to 2020, has greatly benefits the Congolese people as well as the whole world."

-- Congolese Doctor Dennis Mukwege
